What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device—be it a computer, smartphone, or tablet—when you visit a website. These files contain bits of information that help websites perform a variety of functions, from remembering your login details to providing you with personalized content tailored to your preferences. Cookies act as a bridge between your browser and the website, enabling seamless interaction and enhanced functionality.

There are two main types of cookies:

  1. Session Cookies

    These cookies are temporary and exist only for the duration of your browsing session. They are automatically deleted from your device once you close your browser. Session cookies are commonly used to support essential functions, such as navigating between pages or ensuring the security of your interactions on a website. For example, they may keep you logged in as you browse or prevent you from having to re-enter information on every page.

  2. Persistent Cookies

    Unlike session cookies, persistent cookies remain on your device for a predetermined period or until you manually delete them. These cookies are often used to remember your preferences, such as language settings or login credentials, so that your experience on the website is faster and more convenient during future visits. For instance, if you select a preferred currency for an e-commerce site, persistent cookies ensure that preference is saved for your next visit.

Cookies can also be categorized by the party that places them on your device:

  • First-Party Cookies: These are set directly by the website you are visiting and are primarily used to enhance functionality and performance.
  • Third-Party Cookies: These are placed by external services or partners, such as analytics providers or advertisers, and may be used to track user behavior across different websites.

It is important to note that cookies are not inherently harmful. They do not install programs or carry viruses. Instead, they are tools designed to improve the functionality of websites and provide users with a more personalized experience. However, because they involve the collection of certain data, understanding their purpose and management is key to maintaining control over your privacy.

Through the use of cookies, websites can adapt to your preferences, making your online interactions smoother and more efficient. Whether it’s enabling secure transactions, speeding up loading times, or displaying relevant content, cookies are an integral part of today’s digital landscape.
